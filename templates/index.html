Writing

The OET Writing sub-test evaluates healthcare professionals' skills in writing formal letters, like referrals or discharge notes, based on case notes. Candidates must convey clear, accurate, and relevant information using appropriate medical language, focusing on the recipient’s needs and maintaining professionalism.

Listening

The OET Listening sub-test assesses healthcare professionals' ability to understand spoken English in healthcare settings. Candidates listen to recordings of medical dialogues or consultations and answer questions on key information, focusing on comprehension, details, and the ability to follow relevant healthcare conversations.

Practice Listening

Reading

The OET Reading sub-test measures healthcare professionals' ability to comprehend written English in medical contexts. Candidates read various texts, such as articles or reports, and answer questions on main ideas, details, and specific information, demonstrating their ability to understand and interpret relevant healthcare content.
